Glossary

Advantage
A positive effect from a Conflict, Twist, or other rule effect.
Archive, the
An association of librarians, anthropologists, and archaeologists driven to collect and distribute magical knowledge. In this game’s default setup, the PCs are members of the Archive.
Aspect
The ways that magic and the world itself can be described. If Flow (see below) is a verb, aspects are its adverbs.
Attic Lines
Ley Lines that were driven upward long ago and which cascade through the empty space above the sky. Here the world is less changing and there is nothing to disrupt their flow. They are the most reliable way to transport highly aspected long distances.
Canon of Form
The catalog of objects, plants, animals, and archetypes that provides the shapes for conjured things. Individuals have their own Signature Canon formed by experience and upbringing, while regions have a Consensus Canon formed by the city-wide communal subconscious.
Communion-nature
The part of human nature that seeks to connect with others.
Complication
A negative result from a Conflict, Twist, or other rule effect.
Dream Realms
Worlds where concepts and ideals, as well as actual dreams, form the basis of reality. Whereas the Worlds Beyond are persistent, the Dream Realms exist only while there are minds to create them. The Dream Realms are invisible but ever-present – they exist between all living beings. Entering them is the domain of the Communion Nature.
Effect
A Complication or Advantage.
Flow or The Flow
The power you tap into to create magical effects, which wells up into the Great Basin in all places, through all things.
Fount
One of the sources of magical power, each with its own unique character and approaches. The Founts described in this book are Alchemy, Arete, Earthpower, Elementalism, Glory, Necromancy, and Oneiromancy.
Gift, the
Broadly, the ability to do magic. More specifically, the moment about 400 years ago when everyone in the Kaleidoscope was simultaneously granted that ability.
GM Character
A character controlled by the Game Master rather than by the players. Also referred to as an NPC (non-player character).
Great Basin
The world in which most of Sorcerously Advanced takes place. Part of the Kaleidoscope (see below).
Import
A measure of a character’s importance to the plot. The higher your Power score, the lower your Import will be.
Industry-nature
The part of human nature that seeks to build and create.
Interregnum
The period of war and chaos that followed the Gift. The Interregnum was named to imply that, for the civilizations that have passed through it, a more enlightened and peaceful age has arrived. In the estimation of most historians, the Interregnum has not ended.
Kaleidoscope, the
The myriad of worlds, each of which is a coequal counterpart of the Great Basin. These can be reached via Travel (q.v.).
Ley Lines
Rivers of flow that move through the Basin. Ley lines can be tapped, redirected, or fractured into many smaller streams, all of which takes a fair amount of effort and know-how.
Mystery-nature
The part of human nature that seeks to understand the world.
Nature
The six components of one's identity that shape how magic is created. The natures are Communion, Industry, Mystery, Self, Trickery, and War. Natures have both a numerical score (used for character-level actions) and an Approach descriptor (used for player-level actions).
Null Zone
A place where very little flow enters the Basin, and magical tasks are difficult or impossible to accomplish.
Path
One of the many means by which magical effects can be created. Examples include receiving power funneled from a supernatural patron, combining and applying reagents, or chanting words of power.
Player Character
A character controlled by a player, rather than one controlled by the GM. Also sometimes called a “main character”. Player characters have access to Twists; GM characters do not.
Power
A combined rating of a character’s magical prowess, determined by a combination of Nature scores and Expertise. The trade-off between Power and Import is the central balance of the game.
Red Space, the
A vast space parallel to all points in the Kaleidoscope, used for storage.
Reserve
Points that can be spent to bring more of your your character’s abilities to bear on a project. Each character has a maximum number of Reserve points equal to their Power score.
Self-nature
The part of human nature that seeks to understand and change itself.
Soul Repository
A place or device that attracts the souls of those who die in the local area, retaining them in the Great Basin rather than allowing them to continue on to an afterlife in the Worlds Beyond.
Travel
The opening of doors into a bridge-filled space that connects all physical worlds. Travel is the fastest and most reliable means of crossing large distances.
Trickery-nature
The part of human nature that seeks to deceive others.
Twist
An out-of-game currency spent through one of your character’s Nature to generate Effects, affecting the plot in specific ways. You start each session with a number of Twists equal to your Import, and they are generally gained as bad things happen to your character.
Uncountable Spinners, the
A broken system that tethers souls to the Basin with invisible silver cords.
Universal Repository
One of several omnipresent knowledge stores. Each Fount has its own, and its own way of storing knowledge within it.
Unruly Lands
The pejorative used by the civilizations detailed in this book to refer to other civilizations.
War-nature
The part of human nature that seeks to dominate and destroy others.
Worlds Beyond
The supernatural realms beyond the Basin. Each has a series of realms that are commanded by various rulers, often called “pantheons”. The Worlds Beyond sometimes touch the Basin, and sometimes must be reached via Travel. Examples include the Hell of the Sovereigns of Hell, and the Elemental Realms tapped by the Elementalism fount.
